Chicken Tikka

Chicken Tikka is a popular North Indian starter, where boneless chicken pieces are coated with a spicy marinade and then grilled to perfection.

Tikka came from the Turkish word ‘Tike,’ which means ‘Pieces.’

This Indian chicken snack recipe is everyone’s favourite and is a part of the menu of almost every Indian restaurant all over the world.

The Indian chicken tikka was traditionally made in a clay oven called ‘Tandoor.’ But you can make it in an oven, microwave, air fryer, or outdoor grill.

Ingredients Required

2.Salt

3.Ginger garlic paste

4.Garam masala powder

5.Tandoori masala

12.Ghee

Recipe

Rinse 26 oz (750 g) of skinless and boneless chicken breasts (or thighs) with water and pat them dry using paper tissues. Cut them into ½-inch cubes. Marinate The Chicken (First Marinade)

Add chicken cubes 1 teaspoon salt 2 teaspoon ginger garlic paste 2 tablespoon freshly squeezed lime juice To a large mixing bowl and mix well. Cover the bowl with a lid or plastic wrap and refrigerate the chicken for 3-4 hours or overnight. Do The Second Marination Take out the bowl from the refrigerator.

Add 3 tablespoon thick yogurt 1 tablespoon cornstarch 3 tablespoon mustard oil 1 tablespoon tandoori masala powder 3 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder ½ teaspoon garam masala powder ½ teaspoon black peeper powder to the bowl and mix well to coat the chicken pieces.

Keep the marinated chicken in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes or up to 2 days.

Grill The Chicken

In An Oven Preheat the oven to 400℉ (200℃). Thread the marinated chicken in the skewers along with onions and green peppers (capsicum). Line the skewers on a foil-lined baking tray. Grill for 10-12 minutes. Pull the tray from the oven and brush the skewers generously with salted butter. Flip and brush with butter on the other side as well. Return the tray to the oven and grill for another 10-12 minutes. Change the oven setting to BROIL and broil the skewers for 3-4 minutes until they are slightly charred.

Remove the tikka from the oven and serve hot.

Butter Chicken

1 tablespoon sugar

Place them in a bowl and cover them with hot water. Let them soak for about 20-30 minutes, until softened. Once the chilies are softened, drain them and transfer them to a blender. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. If the soup is too thick, you can add more water to reach your desired consistency. Burritos

Burritos are a popular Mexican dish consisting of a flour tortilla wrapped around various fillings, typically including beans, rice, meat, cheese, and vegetables. Originating in northern Mexico, burritos have become a beloved staple in Mexican cuisine and have gained popularity worldwide. IngredIents: Flour Tortillas: Large, soft flour tortillas are traditionally used for wrapping the fillings. Protein: Common protein options include seasoned beef, chicken, pork, or shrimp. Vegetarian alternatives like beans or tofu are also popular. Beans: Refried beans or black beans are commonly used to add flavor and texture. Rice: Mexican-style rice, often flavored with tomatoes, onions, and spices, adds bulk to the burrito. Cheese: Shredded cheese, such as cheddar or Monterey Jack, is sprinkled over the fillings for added richness. Vegetables: Sautéed bell peppers, onions, lettuce, tomatoes, and avocado are popular vegetable options for burritos. Salsa: Fresh salsa or pico de gallo adds a vibrant, tangy flavor to the burrito. Sour Cream: Creamy sour cream is often used as a topping for burritos, adding a cool contrast to the spicy fillings. Guacamole: Creamy, mashed avocado mixed with lime juice, cilantro, and seasonings provides a rich and flavorful addition. How to Make MexIcan BurrItos: Prepare Fillings: Cook your choice of protein (beef, chicken, etc.) with Mexican spices until fully cooked. Prepare rice according to package instructions and season with spices like cumin, chili powder, and garlic. Heat refried beans or black beans in a saucepan. Warm Tortillas: Heat the flour tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave for a few seconds until warm and pliable Assamble burritos: Lay a warm tortilla flat on a clean surface. Spoon a portion of the cooked protein, rice, beans, cheese, vegetables, salsa, sour cream, and guacamole down the center of the tortilla. Wrap Burritos: Fold the sides of the tortilla over the fillings, then fold the bottom edge up and over the fillings, and continue rolling until the burrito is tightly wrapped. Serve: Place the wrapped burritos seam side down on a serving platter. You can serve them whole or cut them in half diagonally for easier eating. Optional: If desired, you can lightly grill or toast the assembled burritos in a skillet or panini press for a crispy exterior. Enjoy: Serve the burritos with additional salsa, sour cream, and guacamole on the side, and enjoy your delicious homemade Mexican burritos
